Our Beth’s First Year

Psalms 65:11 “You crown the year with your goodness; your carts overflow with plenty.”

Our baby daughter’s growth and development during the first year was both delightful and challenging (with all the crying, feeding, changing diapers, sleepless nights and grumpy days). All these experiences were worth, even though they were demanding.

Bethany is a very charm full, strong will baby and a wonderful first addition to our family.She was born on March 19th 2017 at 10pm weighing 7lbs 5oz, 21 inches tall. That’s when we first experienced the joy of parenting a child. It’s really a big blessing to experience this joy after waiting long 9 months. But once we heard Beth’s first cry, we forgot the challenges of pre-born days.

We had been seeing so many changes day after day, weeks after weeks and month after month. But the things that really amazed us were Beth’s love for music (she used to frequently go to sleep with a local gospel song by Azeb), her walking without crawling and imitating actions.

Beth’s First-Year Milestones

  1. First Month: Cries a lot, wakes up every now and then, stares when someone makes new moves with their hand
  2. Second Month: She started smiling; she was able to lift her heads during tummy time.
  3. Third Month: Started recognizing mom.
  4. Fourth Month: Started saying dadddddy.
  5. Fifth Month: Started raising her arms when she wants to be picked up; crying when mom leaves the room.
  6. Sixth Month: Started sitting without any help; giggling when dad or mom makes silly moves; started eating her feet.
  7. Seventh Month: Became very attentive to any movements around her.
  8. Eighth Month: Standing with little help; her first thanksgiving, her first road trip to Indianapolis.
  9. Ninth Month: Standing is no more a problem and of course her first Christmas.
  10. Tenth Month: Started walking with one hand held.
  11. Eleventh Month: Walked unassisted with frequent stumbling.
  12. Twelfth Month: Walked fully .
